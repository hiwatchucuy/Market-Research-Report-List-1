<p><p>The Underground Utility Locator market comprises various technologies for detecting buried utilities. Electromagnetic induction relies on radio waves to identify metallic structures. Radio detection uses frequencies to locate wires indirectly. Acoustic locating employs sound waves to detect non-metallic pipes. Ground penetrating radar (GPR) sends radar pulses to visualize subsurface features. Magnetic locators detect disturbances in the Earth's magnetic field caused by metal objects. Wires and markers provide visual aids for utility identification. Other methods include surveys, sonar, and advanced imaging techniques.</p></p>
