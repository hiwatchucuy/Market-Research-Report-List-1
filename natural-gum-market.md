<p><p>The natural gum market is divided into two primary types: food grade and industry grade. Food grade natural gums are sourced and processed to meet safety standards for human consumption, commonly used as thickening agents, stabilizers, or emulsifiers in various food products. In contrast, industry grade natural gums are utilized in non-food applications, such as adhesives, paints, and textiles, where purity requirements are less stringent. Both markets cater to distinct consumer needs, driving demand across various sectors.</p></p>
