<p><p>The Central Vascular Access Catheter market encompasses several types, including Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters (PICC), Centrally Inserted Catheters (which can be non-tunnelled or tunnelled), and Implanted Ports. PICCs are inserted through a peripheral vein, providing long-term access. Non-tunnelled catheters are temporarily placed directly into central veins, while tunnelled catheters are surgically placed and designed for prolonged use. Implanted ports provide a discreet, long-term access solution under the skin, minimizing infection risks. Other types may include various specialized devices catered to specific medical needs.</p></p>
